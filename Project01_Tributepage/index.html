QUAID-E-AZAM MUHAMMAD ALI JINNAH

The Founder of Pakistan, A Legendery Lawyer, A Great Statesman.

Jinnah studied law at Lincoln's Inn in London. He became the youngest Indian called to the bar at just 19 years old.

He initially advocated for Hindu-Muslim unity before realizing Muslims needed an independent political voice.

Through flawless legal arguments, he united millions of people to demand a separate, free sovereign state.

In August 1947, he became the first Governor-General of Pakistan, establishing the foundations of the country.

Quick Profile Facts:

  • Full Name: Muhammad Ali Jinnah
  • Popular Titles: Quaid-e-Azam (Great Leader) & Baba-e-Qaum (Father of the Nation)
  • Born: December 25, 1876 (Karachi)
  • Died: September 11, 1948 (Karachi)
  • Resting Place: Mazar-e-Quaid, Karachi


Major Life Achievements (Timeline):

  1. 1895 - The Youngest Barrister: At just 19 years of age, he qualified in law from London's Lincoln's Inn and became the youngest Indian to be called to the bar.
  2. 1906 - Entry into Politics: He started his political career with the Indian National Congress. In his early days, he was called the Ambassador of Hindu-Muslim Unity.
  3. 1913 - Joining the Muslim League: He joined the All-India Muslim League so that a constitutional struggle could be fought for the rights of the Muslims of the sub-continent.
  4. 1929 - The Famous 14 Points: He presented his famous '14 Points' to safeguard the political and constitutional rights of Muslims.
  5. 1940 - Lahore Resolution: Under his leadership, the Lahore Resolution was passed on March 23, 1940, which made the destination of the creation of Pakistan absolutely clear.
  6. 1947 - Freedom & First Governor-General: Thanks to his unparalleled struggle, Pakistan came into existence on August 14, 1947, and he took the oath as the country's first Governor-General.


The Three Golden Principles:

Quaid-e-Azam gave three golden principles to make the nation strong:

  • Faith (Yakeen-e-Muhkam)
  • Unity (Ittehad)
  • Discipline (Tanzheem)
